🌰 Meet Sprout

A tiny seed is tucked into the soil. It looks plain on the outside... but something amazing is packed inside!

Sprout says

"Hi there! I'm Sprout — well, right now I'm just a seed, resting in the soil. I might look plain, but I'm carrying everything I need to grow!"

📖 What's inside a seed? If you cut a seed open, you'd see two important parts: a seed coat that protects what's inside, and a seed leaf that stores food for the young plant. Let's take a look inside Sprout!

🔍 Explore: Inside a Seed

Tap each part of the seed below to find out what it is and what it does.

👆 Tap the outer rim or the inside to begin
✓Seed Coat

This is the tough outer layer of the seed. It protects the delicate parts inside from damage, insects, and drying out.

✓Seed Leaf

This stores food for the young plant. Before the plant has its own true leaves, the seed leaf keeps it fed and growing.
